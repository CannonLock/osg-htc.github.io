Power your Research with
distributed High Throughput Computing

The OSG is here to support researchers with HTC workloads. We provide open-source software, compute resources, and services for researchers and research organizations to support their dHTC compute requirements.

Learn how the OSG facilitates dHTC
The OSG is a consortium of research collaborations, campuses, national laboratories and software providers dedicated to the advancement of all open science via the practice of distributed High Throughput Computing (dHTC), and the advancement of its state of the art.

Submit Locally, Run Globally

OSG Access Points provide researchers with a location to place their HTC workloads and data, then submit to a associated computing resource.

Don't have access to a Access Point?

OSGConnect provides researchers with an OSG operated Access Point. Using this Access Point researchers can submit jobs to the OSPool, an OSG operated pool of shared computing and data capacity.

OSG makes HTC workloads accessible to all Researchers

OSG services allow researchers of all fields of science to ask bigger questions. OSG Access Points provide entrypoint into The Open Science Pool provides access compute resources for complex workloads. The Open Science Data Federation facilitates the use of large data sets.

New to Large-Scale Computing?

OSG provides a dedicated staff of Research Facilitators here to help you use our services. Research Facilitators can assist with your computational approach, teach you how to place your workloads and data on our Access Points, and connect you with researchers using similar software or methods.
